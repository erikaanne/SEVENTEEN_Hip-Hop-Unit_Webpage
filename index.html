INTRODUCTION

The Hip-Hop Team is one of SEVENTEEN’s three sub-units, made up of S.Coups, Wonwoo, Mingyu, and Vernon.
These four aren’t just rappers—they’re storytellers, performers, and creative minds who bring a whole new energy to SEVENTEEN’s music.
With their distinct rap styles, they add depth and personality to every song they touch. S.Coups, as the leader,
delivers powerful and commanding verses, Wonwoo brings a thoughtful, poetic touch to his lyrics, Mingyu blends charisma
with smooth flows, and Vernon adds a fresh mix of playful and introspective wordplay.
Together, they create a dynamic and balanced unit that keeps fans hooked.

What makes the Hip-Hop Team special is how effortlessly they mix different styles while staying true to SEVENTEEN’s sound.
They don’t just focus on heavy beats and fast verses—they bring real emotions into their music.
Songs like Trauma and Back It Up show how versatile they are, switching between deep, introspective tracks and high-energy bangers.
But beyond their talent, their chemistry and friendship make them even more fun to watch.
Whether they’re hyping each other up on stage or joking around off-camera, their bond makes every performance feel even more powerful.
The Hip-Hop Team isn’t just about rap—it’s about passion, creativity, and connection, and that’s what makes them unforgettable.

DISCOGRAPHY

Since their debut, SEVENTEEN’s Hip-Hop Unit has continuously evolved, delivering powerful rap performances and diverse musical styles.
Their first official track, "Ah Yeah," from 17 Carat (2015), set the foundation for their unit’s distinct sound.
Over the years, they have explored various themes, from youthful energy in "Mansae" to introspective storytelling in "Trauma."

Their discography includes standout tracks like "Back It Up" from An Ode (2019), the emotional "I Can't Run Away" from Attacca (2021),
and the high-energy "Fire" FML (2023). With each release, they continue to push creative boundaries, solidifying their place in the K-pop industry.
